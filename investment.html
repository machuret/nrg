Tidied things up? Put your equity to work.

Once the home loan’s sharp and the debts are simple, a lot of our clients start eyeing the next move — an investment property. This is the step you take when you’re ready, and we’ll make sure the lending is structured to suit it.

The essentials

What’s different about investment lending.

Using your equity

The equity you’ve built in your own home can often become the deposit for an investment — no need to save from scratch. We work out how much you can responsibly access without overstretching the household.

Rental income counts

Lenders include a portion of expected rent when assessing what you can borrow — usually around 70–80%. That changes the maths from a standard home loan, and we factor it in properly.

Loan structure matters

Interest-only vs principal-and-interest, offset accounts, keeping investment and home debt separate — how the loan is set up affects flexibility and your accountant’s job at tax time.

Different rates and rules

Investment loans often price and assess a little differently to owner-occupier loans. With 40+ lenders on the panel, we find the one whose investor policy actually fits your plan.

At a high level

The tax side, in broad strokes.

Investment property comes with tax angles — the difference between negative and positive gearing, deductible interest and costs, depreciation, and capital gains down the track. These can meaningfully change the real cost of holding a property.

We’ll make sure the lending is structured so your accountant has clean lines to work with. But the tax advice itself is their job, not ours.

Please note: NRG provides credit assistance, not tax or financial advice. Always confirm the tax treatment of any investment with a registered tax agent or accountant before you proceed.
